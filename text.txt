Question 1:
What is the motivation behind parameter control in Evolutionary Algorithms?
A. To increase the population size
B. To reduce the mutation rate
C. To find optimal parameter values for better performance during the search
D. To select better individuals for the offspring population

Answer: C

Question 2:
Which of the following is NOT a secondary feature of parameter control in Evolutionary Algorithms?
A. Evidence/data backing up changes
B. Level/scope of change
C. What component is controlled
D. None of the above

Answer: C

Question 3:
What are the primary features that distinguish various forms of parameter control in Evolutionary Algorithms?
A. Evidence and level/scope of change
B. What component is changed and how the change is made
C. Progress made and diversity of the population
D. None of the above

Answer: B

Question 4:
What are the three major types of parameter control in Evolutionary Algorithms?
A. Deterministic, fixed and relative
B. Adaptive, fixed and relative
C. Self-adaptive, fixed and relative
D. Deterministic, adaptive and self-adaptive

Answer: D

Question 5:
What are the different levels at which the parameter may take effect in parameter control in Evolutionary Algorithms?
A. Population, individual and sub-individual
B. Mutation rate, selection pressure and crossover rate
C. Environment, fitness function and evaluation function
D. None of the above

Answer: A
